# Chekafgham-Retail-Sales-Performance-Profitability-Investigation 

** Author ** Emmanuel Okafor I.
** Date ** 2025-12-20


## Project Backgroud
Chekafgham Retail is currently reviewing the perfoemance of our retail business following noticeable fluctuations in revenue, customer behavior and product profitability across multiple categories.
The Executive Team has raised concerns that while sales volumn appears stable, profit margins and customer value may be undeperforming. Additionally, we lack clarity on who our most valuable customers are, which products truly drive profit, and when is sales peak during the year.
The project analyzes available transactional dataset and produces an interactive Excel dashboard with actionable recommendations.



## Project Objective
- Investigate if the revenue growth is translating into real profit, whether categories with high sale volume deliver strong  profitability and which customer deliver the most revenue and repeat purchases.
- Determine how Demographics affect sales, sale trends over time and whether customers typically make bulk purchase or small frequent ones.
- Build an interactive Excel dashboard to inform board level decisions.
- Recommend prioritized actions to enhance sale performance profitability opotimization.



  ## Datasets
  ** Retail Sales.csv ** -- transaction_id, customer_id, gender, age, category, quantity, price_per_unit, total_sale, cogs, sale_date, sale_time.
  Data cleaning and transction was carried out to
  (i) Extract month (text) from sale_date
  (ii) Ensure that gender column has all entries harmonize to Male or Female
  (iii) Use Average age for null values.


  ## Dashboard Features
  - Key KPIs: Total order, Revenue per Customer, Age group, Total revenue, Avg. item per order, Avg.spent per order, Revenue by period type.
  - Visuals: Revenue Vs Gross profit, Categories for investment, Most valuable customers, Demographics Purchase Behavior, Sales trend over period type, Bulk Vs Small frequent buyer.
  - Interactivity: Cusor on Heat map for more information.


    ## Key Findings
    - If revenue is rising but gross margin is shrinking, cost are eroding profitability.
    - From question 1 chart, both revenue, gross profit and gross margin line rises and the gap between them widens. This means the growth is healthy and in turn means profitable sales.
    - For category, clothing recorded highest gross profit and gross margin.
    - Clothing and Electronics deliver strong profitability.
    - The top 10 customers who spend most are customers_id: 1,2,3,4,5,54,55,71,84,87 in that order.
    - The age group 36-50(male & female) generate most revenue while age group 61+(male & female) generate least revenue.
    - Female between 26-60 yrs generate more revenue than other gender and age brackets.
    - The retail shop make most money(from peak downward) at:
    - Monthly: December, then September, October. The least revenue at Febuary.
    - Hourly: 19th hr, then 21. The least revenue at 23rd hr.
    - Weekend: The highest revenue was recorded on 22/11/24.


      ## Recommendations
      - Better pricing and operational efficiency should be maintained and improved on for profitable sales.
      - Clothing and Electronics categories deserve more investment from marketing bugdet.
      - The top 10 customers who spend most must be given soecial considerations like loyalty programs, targeted marketing.
      - Customer ids 1,2,3,4,5,are your VIPs; 54,55,71,84,87 may be occassional big spenders.
      - Age bracket 36-50(male & female) should be given highest priority for premium promotions and product offerings. Next to this is female between 26-60 yrs.
      - Age group 61+ will need  tailored promotions to encourage trial or bundles.
      - Staffing and inventory should be optimized in period type(Month, Hour, Weekend) with most sales.
      - Most customer typically made small frequent purchases than bulk purchases.
